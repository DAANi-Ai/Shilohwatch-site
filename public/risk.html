Past Performance and Backtesting

Past performance is not a reliable indicator of future results. This applies to all historical data, analytics, and backtested results shown on the platform.

Backtesting results are generated by applying analytical rules to historical market data. They have important limitations:

  • They don't account for slippage, commissions, or the impact of your own order size on price
  • Historical data may contain errors or gaps
  • Market conditions that produced past patterns may not repeat
  • Backtested strategies can be subject to "overfitting" to historical data, performing worse in live conditions
  • They don't reflect the psychological challenge of following a strategy in real market conditions

Backtesting results shown on Shiloh Watch are for educational and analytical purposes only. They're not a predictor of live trading performance.
